The CLEAR Framework — Your writing toolkit for process texts
Book-based
Use this framework every time you write a process explanation. Keep this page open during Production.
C
Choose the exact action verb
Pick a precise verb — not a general one. Separate not put. Tighten not fix. Pour not move.
L
Label the sequence position
Open every step with a sequencing marker: First, Next, Then, After that, Finally.
E
Explain the action briefly
One sentence maximum. Keep it direct. Simple present tense: Separate / Filter / Tighten — not "You should separate".
A
Add necessary detail
Specify: time, tool, quantity, direction, or colour. "Pour 500ml of water into the green bin" — not "pour some water".
R
Review for clarity
Ask: could a stranger in a different city follow this without asking questions? If not, add detail.
Worked example — environment topic
Topic: How to separate household waste
First, separate your household waste into three categories.
Next, place all food scraps and organic waste into the green bin.
After that, fold cardboard boxes flat and insert them into the blue recycling bin.
Then, compress any plastic bottles before placing them in the yellow bin.
Finally, tighten the lid on each bin and move them to the collection point before 6 AM.

Sequencing language — from opening to closing a process
Reference
Before you begin, ...
↓
First, ...
↓
Next, ...
or
After that, ...
↓
Then, ...
↓
In the last step, ...
↓
Finally, ...
Required
Optional addition
Avoid: Passive voice ("The lid should be tightened" → write "Tighten the lid"), vague quantities ("some", "a bit", "around"), and uncertainty words ("maybe", "kind of").

How to Compost at Home

Before you begin, prepare a container with small holes in the base to allow drainage. First, layer a base of dry leaves or shredded cardboard at the bottom of the bin. Next, add a 5cm layer of food scraps — vegetable peelings, coffee grounds, or fruit waste. After that, cover the food scraps with another layer of dry material. Then, insert a long stick or pipe into the centre to allow air to circulate. Finally, seal the lid and place the bin in a shaded area. After four to six weeks, remove the finished compost from the base and spread it around your plants.
