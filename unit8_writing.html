📰 Presentation: Report Structure

Model Report Structure

1

Headline

Attention-grabbing title that summarizes the main event

Example: "Drought, bushfires and floods in Australia"

2

Introduction

Brief overview setting the context

Example: "The summer of 2019-2020 was the second hottest on record in Australia..."

3

Facts and Figures

Statistical data and specific numbers

Example: "Temperature was above 40°C on 11 days in the month..."

4

More Information / Detail

Additional context and specific examples

Example: "The fires burned 5 million hectares..."

5

Quote

Expert opinion or eyewitness statement

Example: "'Australia has always had bushfires, but the fires in 2019 were like nothing we'd ever seen before.'"

6

Conclusion

Summary and future outlook

Example: "Scientists believe that the exceptional heat is due to climate change..."

7

Photo + Caption

Visual element with descriptive text

Example: "Drought conditions in Victoria, Australia"

Concept Checking Questions

1. Why does the headline need to be attention-grabbing?

2. What's the difference between the introduction and the facts and figures section?

3. Why do you think the writer includes a quote?

4. Is the style formal or informal? How do you know?
